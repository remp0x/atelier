# Staking (real-yield)

<StatusBadge status="Beta" />

$ATELIER staking is a real-yield, revenue-share program: stake $ATELIER and earn a pro-rata share
of platform revenue paid in USDC, not inflationary token emissions.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Not audited, not on mainnet">
Staking is in testing on Solana devnet and has not been professionally audited. It is not live on
mainnet and there is no date for that yet -- a third-party audit is a hard gate before any
mainnet deployment. Nothing on this page describes a live, funded product. Treat it as a preview
of the design, not something you can stake into today.
</Callout>

## How it works

The staking program is non-custodial: a program-owned PDA holds both the staked-$ATELIER vault
and the USDC reward vault. No admin key can move funds out of either vault -- only a user's own
`unstake` and `claim` instructions can.

### Lock tiers

Staking $ATELIER means locking it into one of three tiers, each with a different reward
multiplier:

| Tier | Lock | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| 30-day | 30-day lock | 1x |
| 90-day | 90-day lock | 4x |
| 180-day | 180-day lock | 8x |

A longer lock means a larger weighted share of the reward pool for the same amount staked, but
your principal is committed for that lock's duration.

### Rewards drip linearly, they are not claim-anytime-instant

This is not a pool where rewards accrue continuously and sit ready to claim the instant they're
funded. Each funding round is distributed using a Synthetix-style linear drip: USDC funded into
the reward vault pays out gradually over a fixed `reward_duration` window, pro-rata to each
staker's weighted share (amount staked x tier multiplier) for the time they were staked during
that window. Claiming pulls whatever has dripped so far -- it does not fast-forward the window.

### Reward source and funding cadence

Rewards come from platform revenue, not new token issuance. By default, 50% of creator-fee
revenue (configurable) is routed into the staking reward vault, funded on a weekly cadence from
treasury USDC.

### Principal

Unstaking returns your staked $ATELIER 1:1. Staking never risks your principal the way, say,
[Earn's](/docs/concepts/earn) liquidity-provision venue does -- the risk profile here is smart
contract and audit risk on an unaudited program, not market/trading risk on your stake.
