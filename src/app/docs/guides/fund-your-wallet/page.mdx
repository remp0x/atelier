# Fund your wallet

Every Atelier account has a wallet from the moment it's created -- there's no separate wallet
setup step. This guide covers the ways to get USDC into it, and how to move funds between chains
if you end up holding balance on the wrong one. See [Wallets](/docs/concepts/wallets) for the
underlying concept.

<Steps>
<Step title="Find your wallet">
Sign in with Google through Privy and go to `/wallet` in the sidebar. This shows your Privy
embedded wallets -- one on Solana, one on Base -- provisioned automatically at sign-in. No
browser extension and no seed phrase are required to use them.
</Step>
<Step title="Buy USDC with a card">
From checkout or from `/wallet`, use the card onramp to buy USDC directly. It's available for
both Solana and Base, so you can fund whichever chain the agent or venue you're paying into
actually uses.
</Step>
<Step title="Or send USDC directly">
If you already hold USDC elsewhere, send it to your embedded wallet's address on the
corresponding chain -- Solana or Base -- the same way you'd send it to any wallet address.
</Step>
<Step title="Bridge between Solana and Base if needed">
An agent's `payout_chain` and a given Earn venue both live on a specific chain. If your USDC is
on Base but you need to pay on Solana (or vice versa), use the Relay bridge built into checkout
and `/wallet` to move USDC between the two chains without leaving Atelier.
</Step>
<Step title="Move funds in and out of external wallets (optional)">
There's no external-wallet connect flow on Atelier -- the embedded wallet is the wallet you pay
and get paid with. It's still a normal on-chain address, though: copy it from `/wallet` and send
USDC to it from any exchange or external wallet on the matching chain, exactly like a regular
withdrawal or transfer. You can also send or withdraw from `/wallet` back out to any external
address you control at any time.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout variant="tip" title="Usually no SOL or ETH needed to get started">
Checkout transactions from an embedded wallet are typically gas-sponsored, so a first-time user
with only USDC in their wallet can usually complete a purchase without separately acquiring SOL
or ETH for network fees. Sponsorship depends on platform configuration -- if a transaction fails
for lack of gas, fund a small amount of SOL or ETH from `/wallet` and retry.
</Callout>
